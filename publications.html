Publications

Neural Architecture Search with Reinforcement Learning for Image Classification

NeurIPS 2024 A*
A. Petrov · M. Sokolov · E. Ivanova

We propose a novel approach to neural architecture search using reinforcement learning techniques. Our method automatically discovers high-performing CNN architectures for image classification tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results while reducing search time by 40%.

Deep Learning Computer Vision 2024

Convergence Properties of Adaptive Optimization Methods in Non-Convex Settings

ICML 2024 A*
N. Lebedev · K. Smirnov · O. Popova

Rigorous mathematical analysis of convergence guarantees for modern optimization algorithms in deep learning. We establish theoretical bounds for adaptive methods like Adam and RMSprop in non-convex settings with practical implications.

Deep Learning Optimization 2024

Self-Supervised Learning for Visual Representations with Contrastive Methods

CVPR 2023 A*
T. Kozlov · I. Novikov · S. Morozov

Novel approaches to learning robust visual features without labeled data using contrastive learning and geometric transformations. Our method achieves superior performance on downstream tasks with minimal fine-tuning.

Computer Vision Self-Supervised 2023

Efficient Transformers for Natural Language Understanding Tasks

ACL 2023 A
D. Volkov · V. Fedorov · A. Kuznetsov

Introducing efficient transformer architectures that reduce computational complexity while maintaining performance on natural language understanding benchmarks. Achieves 3x speedup with comparable accuracy to standard models.

NLP Transformers 2023

Theoretical Foundations of Deep Reinforcement Learning in Continuous Spaces

ICLR 2022 A*
E. Ivanova · M. Sokolov · A. Petrov

Comprehensive theoretical analysis of deep reinforcement learning algorithms in continuous action and state spaces. We provide convergence guarantees and sample complexity bounds for policy gradient methods.

Reinforcement Learning Theory 2022

Interpretable Machine Learning: Methods and Applications

AAAI 2022 A
K. Smirnov · N. Lebedev · T. Kozlov

Survey of interpretable machine learning methods with applications to critical domains. We present a unified framework for understanding model decisions and provide practical tools for explanation generation and validation.

Explainable AI Interpretability 2022
