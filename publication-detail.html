Neural Architecture Search with Reinforcement Learning for Image Classification

A. Petrov · M. Sokolov · E. Ivanova
March 15, 2024 · Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems
Deep Learning Computer Vision Neural Architecture Search Reinforcement Learning

Abstract

We propose a novel approach to neural architecture search using reinforcement learning techniques. Our method automatically discovers high-performing CNN architectures for image classification tasks without requiring extensive manual design. The controller network learns to generate architecture descriptions using policy gradient methods, optimizing for both accuracy and computational efficiency.

Experimental results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet demonstrate that our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance while reducing the search time by 40% compared to existing methods. The discovered architectures transfer well to other vision tasks, showing strong generalization capabilities.

Key Contributions

  • Novel reinforcement learning formulation for neural architecture search
  • Efficient search strategy that reduces computational cost by 40%
  • State-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (97.8% accuracy) and ImageNet (Top-1: 79.2%)
  • Strong transfer learning performance across multiple vision tasks
  • Theoretical analysis of convergence properties and search space exploration

Methodology

Our approach uses a recurrent neural network controller that generates architecture descriptions by predicting a sequence of tokens. The controller is trained with REINFORCE to maximize the expected accuracy on a validation set. We introduce several key innovations:

Search Space Design: We define a flexible search space that includes skip connections, different convolution types, and various activation functions. This allows the controller to explore a rich space of possible architectures.

Efficient Training: To reduce the computational cost, we employ parameter sharing and early stopping techniques. Candidate architectures share weights during evaluation, significantly speeding up the search process.

Results

We evaluated our method on multiple benchmark datasets and compared against both manually designed architectures and other automated search methods. The discovered architectures consistently outperform baseline models while maintaining reasonable computational costs.

On CIFAR-10, our best architecture achieves 97.8% test accuracy with 3.2M parameters. On ImageNet, we reach 79.2% top-1 accuracy and 94.6% top-5 accuracy. The search process completes in approximately 4 GPU-days on modern hardware.
