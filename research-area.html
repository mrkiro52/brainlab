Deep Learning Theory

Investigating theoretical foundations of deep neural networks, convergence properties, and generalization bounds

Overview

Deep learning has revolutionized artificial intelligence across numerous domains, yet many fundamental questions about why and how deep neural networks work remain open. Our research group focuses on developing rigorous mathematical frameworks to understand the theoretical underpinnings of deep learning systems.

We investigate convergence properties of training algorithms, generalization capabilities of overparameterized networks, and the role of architecture in learning representations. Our work bridges the gap between empirical success and theoretical understanding, providing both practical insights and fundamental contributions to machine learning theory.

Key Research Directions

Optimization Landscapes

Analyzing the geometry of loss surfaces in deep networks, understanding critical points, and developing provable optimization guarantees for neural network training algorithms.

Generalization Theory

Establishing theoretical bounds on generalization error, investigating the role of implicit regularization, and understanding why overparameterized networks avoid overfitting.

Architecture Design Principles

Deriving theoretical principles for designing neural architectures, analyzing the expressive power of different network structures, and understanding the role of depth and width.

Training Dynamics

Studying the trajectory of gradient-based optimization in neural networks, analyzing convergence rates, and understanding the interplay between learning rate, batch size, and model performance.

Research Methodology

Our approach combines rigorous mathematical analysis with carefully designed experiments. We develop theoretical frameworks grounded in optimization theory, statistical learning theory, and functional analysis, then validate our predictions through controlled empirical studies.

We collaborate closely with practitioners to ensure our theoretical insights address real-world challenges in deep learning. This feedback loop between theory and practice drives our research agenda and ensures relevance to the broader machine learning community.

Neural Architecture Search with Reinforcement Learning for Image Classification

NeurIPS 2024 A*
A. Petrov · M. Sokolov · E. Ivanova

We propose a novel approach to neural architecture search using reinforcement learning techniques. Our method automatically discovers high-performing CNN architectures for image classification tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results while reducing search time by 40%.

Convergence Properties of Adaptive Optimization Methods in Non-Convex Settings

ICML 2024 A*
L. Kuznetsov · O. Fedorova

This work presents a rigorous mathematical analysis of popular adaptive optimization algorithms such as Adam and RMSprop. We establish convergence guarantees under realistic non-convex conditions and provide insights into their behavior in practical deep learning scenarios.

Self-Supervised Learning for Visual Representations with Contrastive Methods

CVPR 2024 A*
N. Volkov · T. Morozova · I. Smirnov

We introduce an improved self-supervised learning framework based on contrastive learning principles. Our approach learns robust visual representations without labels, achieving competitive performance on downstream tasks including object detection and semantic segmentation.
