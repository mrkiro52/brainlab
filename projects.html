Project Overview

This project aims to develop automated neural architecture search methods specifically designed for medical image analysis. We are creating novel algorithms that can discover optimal CNN architectures for tasks such as tumor detection, organ segmentation, and disease classification. Our approach combines reinforcement learning with domain-specific constraints to ensure both high accuracy and clinical interpretability.

The project is funded by the Russian Science Foundation (RSF) and involves collaboration with leading medical institutions in Moscow. We have already achieved significant results, including a 15% improvement in early cancer detection accuracy compared to manually designed architectures.

Project Overview

With the growing demand for on-device AI, this project focuses on developing lightweight transformer architectures that can run efficiently on mobile and edge devices. We are exploring novel attention mechanisms, quantization techniques, and pruning strategies to reduce computational costs while maintaining high performance.

Our models achieve up to 10x speedup compared to standard transformers with minimal accuracy loss. The project has applications in real-time translation, text generation, and image recognition on smartphones. We collaborate with major tech companies to ensure practical deployment of our research.

Project Overview

This ambitious project develops advanced reinforcement learning algorithms for autonomous robots operating in complex, dynamic environments. We focus on sample-efficient learning, safe exploration, and transfer learning across different robotic platforms. Our research addresses key challenges in robot manipulation, navigation, and human-robot collaboration.

The project includes both simulation studies and real-world experiments with industrial robot arms and mobile platforms. We have established partnerships with robotics manufacturers and are working towards deploying our algorithms in warehouse automation and manufacturing scenarios. Our methods have reduced training time by 60% compared to baseline RL approaches.

Project Overview

Financial institutions require AI systems that not only make accurate predictions but can also explain their decisions to regulators and clients. This project develops interpretable machine learning models for credit scoring, fraud detection, and investment analysis. We combine neural networks with symbolic reasoning to create transparent and trustworthy AI systems.

Our approach provides human-understandable explanations while maintaining prediction accuracy competitive with black-box models. The project is conducted in partnership with leading Russian banks and fintech companies, ensuring real-world applicability of our methods.

Project Overview

This completed project developed federated learning frameworks that enable multiple hospitals to collaboratively train AI models without sharing sensitive patient data. Our methods ensure privacy while achieving model performance comparable to centralized training. The project addressed key challenges in communication efficiency, handling non-IID data distributions, and defending against adversarial attacks.

The resulting framework was successfully deployed across five major hospitals in Moscow, training diagnostic models on millions of patient records while maintaining full data privacy. This work has been published in top-tier conferences and has influenced healthcare AI policy discussions.

Project Overview

This project tackled the challenge of building high-quality NLP models for languages with limited labeled data. Using self-supervised learning and transfer learning techniques, we developed methods to leverage large amounts of unlabeled text in low-resource languages. Our approach combines multilingual pre-training with language-specific fine-tuning strategies.

The project successfully created NLP tools for several minority languages of the Russian Federation, including machine translation, sentiment analysis, and named entity recognition systems. Our models are now being used by educational institutions and cultural organizations to preserve and promote linguistic diversity.
